Dear Editors,

Please consider the enclosed manuscript, "The Homeostatic Maintenance of English Countability: Bidirectional Inference and the Stability of Grammatical Clusters," for publication as a research article in *Linguistics*.

The paper argues that English morphosyntactic countability is best modeled as a homeostatic property cluster maintained by bidirectional inference between count morphosyntax and semantic individuation. It aims to explain both the strong clustering of count properties and the constrained dissociation pattern found in quasi-count nouns such as *cattle*, *police*, and *clergy*. The argument combines theoretical analysis with corpus evidence from COCA and a preregistered exploratory LLM judgment probe used only for sparse-cell extrapolation.

I believe the manuscript fits *Linguistics* because it addresses a general problem in grammatical theory, engages current work on lexical and syntactic category structure, and develops empirically testable predictions extending beyond the English case.

This manuscript is original work, has not been published previously, and is not under consideration elsewhere. The submission package includes an anonymized manuscript, a separate title page, and a data-availability statement in the manuscript. Supporting materials for the quantitative components can be supplied through the submission system.

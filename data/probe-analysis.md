# Construction-Sensitivity Probe: Results

## Data source
COCA (Corpus of Contemporary American English), queried 2026-03-30.

## Complete table

| Noun × Det | Existential | Verb-following | Total | E share |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| three people | 128 | 1,356 | 1,484 | 8.6% |
| three folks | 2 | 1 | 3 | sparse |
| three cattle | 0 | ~3 (filtered) | ~3 | sparse |
| several people | 120 | 1,155 | 1,275 | 9.4% |
| several folks | 6 | 24 | 30 | 20.0% |
| several cattle | 0 | 0 | 0 | n/a |
| many people | 1,046 | 29,449 | 30,495 | 3.4% |
| many folks | 18 | 433 | 451 | 4.0% |
| many cattle | 0 | ~11 (raw) | ~11 | 0% |

## Findings

### 1. Hierarchy confirmed
*Three folks* (3 tokens) and *three cattle* (~3 head uses) are vanishingly rare compared to *three people* (1,484). The tight property is massively suppressed for both quasi-count nouns.

### 2. Loose property shows no construction effect (as predicted)
*Many people* existential share: 3.4%. *Many folks* existential share: 4.0%. Difference: 0.6pp. The loose property distributes similarly across constructions for both nouns.

### 3. Construction-sensitivity prediction: inconclusive
*Three folks* has only 3 total tokens — too few to compute a meaningful existential share. The suppression operates at the *three* + *folks* level before any construction effect can be measured. The prediction remains untested.

### 4. New finding: cattle rejects quantified existentials categorically
- `there [be] cattle` (bare): 6 tokens
- `there [be] [three/several/many] cattle`: 0 tokens (across all three determinatives)

Bare *cattle* in existentials is grammatical. Quantified *cattle* in existentials is categorically avoided. This suggests the existential frame amplifies the individuation demand that quantifiers impose. It's evidence against a purely morphological explanation (if *cattle* were just a defective plural, bare and quantified existentials should pattern alike) and for the precision-demand story.

### 5. Several folks: no existential suppression
*Several folks* existential share (20.0%) is actually higher than *several people* (9.4%), though N=30 makes this noisy. No evidence of existential avoidance for the moderate property — consistent with the prediction that construction-sensitivity should be specific to tight properties.

## What this means for the paper

The corpus probe **strengthens the hierarchy** (the frequency data are robust) but **cannot test construction-sensitivity** (the critical cell is too sparse). The paper should:
1. Report the new existential data, especially the cattle bare-vs-quantified finding
2. Compress the unrun acceptability probe to a paragraph of predictions
3. Note that corpus data confirm the hierarchy but are too sparse to test construction effects — this motivates the acceptability study as future work
